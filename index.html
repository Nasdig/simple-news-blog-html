Quantum computing

What is quantum computing?

Quantum computing is based on quantum mechanics and is developed around qubits, which, unlike classical bits (based on zeros and ones), allow for the superposition of states. This feature allows for greater simulation capabilities, resulting in more accurate results.

Unlike a classical computer, a quantum computer is built on an internal structure composed of atoms and various components that make such calculations possible.

Now then, what is quantum mechanics based on? It is part of quantum physics. And why is it important to know this? Because in order to understand the internal structure of a quantum computer, one must have knowledge of physics and quantum mechanics. This is not the case with classical computing, where basic knowledge of electronics and some logic is usually enough to understand how it works.

Software programming for these computers is still basic, but quantum programming languages already exist. The theory of quantum mechanics was introduced in 1879, and based on this theoretical foundation, companies like Google and IBM began developing their quantum computers.
